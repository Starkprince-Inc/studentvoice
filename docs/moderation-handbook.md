# Editorial and moderation handbook

## Language

Describe observable conduct neutrally. Use “the recording appears to show…” and attribute every disputed claim. Do not call a person a criminal, goon, perpetrator, or similar label without a final authoritative basis that counsel approves.

## Required review

1. Confirm rights basis and preserve the original hash receipt.
2. Verify the timestamp against the original and working-proxy map.
3. Review redactions frame by frame, including transitions and motion blur.
4. Require two independent reviewer approvals for an allegation.
5. Require editor approval for publication.
6. For a public name, additionally require readable badge/nameplate evidence or an official record, a second independent source, and recorded legal approval.

Contradictions remain visible as attributed counterclaims. Reviewers must not resolve uncertainty by majority sentiment. Every named subject receives a right-of-reply invitation, appeal path, and visible response status.

## Corrections

Never silently edit an allegation. Retract urgently when continued display creates an acute safety risk, then preserve a restricted audit copy. Otherwise publish a new immutable version with the reason, prior wording, replacement wording, time, and approver.
